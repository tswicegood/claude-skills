# Context Adaptation Guide

This reference provides guidance on tailoring book reports to different project contexts.

## Detecting Project Context

Infer the project context from:
- Project name and description
- Conversation history and topics discussed
- Project Knowledge documents
- User's work or interests mentioned in conversation

Common contexts: professional/business, personal development, academic, hobby/interest-specific.

## Adaptation Strategies by Context

### Professional/Business Context

**Immediate Action Items:**
- Focus on business implementation and ROI
- Emphasize scalability and team application
- Connect to organizational goals and metrics
- Include change management considerations

**Strategic Applications:**
- Link to business strategy and competitive advantage
- Consider organizational culture fit
- Address stakeholder perspectives
- Think about cross-functional implications

**Personalized Recommendations:**
- Suggest complementary books relevant to their industry
- Reference business leaders or case studies from their sector
- Consider their organizational role (leadership, IC, manager)

### Personal Development Context

**Immediate Action Items:**
- Focus on individual habits and routines
- Emphasize personal reflection and journaling
- Include mindset shifts and self-awareness
- Make actions concrete and measurable

**Strategic Applications:**
- Connect to life goals and values
- Consider work-life integration
- Address emotional and psychological aspects
- Think about long-term personal growth

**Personalized Recommendations:**
- Suggest books that complement their growth journey
- Reference personal development frameworks
- Consider their life stage and circumstances

### Academic/Research Context

**Immediate Action Items:**
- Focus on research applications and methodologies
- Emphasize critical thinking and analysis
- Include connections to academic frameworks
- Make actions research-oriented

**Strategic Applications:**
- Link to theoretical frameworks and models
- Consider epistemological implications
- Address methodological considerations
- Think about interdisciplinary connections

**Personalized Recommendations:**
- Suggest academic papers or scholarly works
- Reference relevant researchers or schools of thought
- Consider their field of study

### Hobby/Interest Context

**Immediate Action Items:**
- Focus on enjoyment and experimentation
- Emphasize practical skills and techniques
- Include creative applications
- Make actions fun and engaging

**Strategic Applications:**
- Connect to skill mastery and progression
- Consider community and social aspects
- Address personal meaning and fulfillment
- Think about creative expression

**Personalized Recommendations:**
- Suggest books that deepen their specific interest
- Reference practitioners or experts in their hobby
- Consider their skill level and goals

## Tone Adjustments

### Professional
- Formal but approachable
- Results-oriented language
- Data-driven insights
- Action verb focus

### Personal
- Warm and encouraging
- Growth-mindset language
- Introspective questions
- Motivational tone

### Academic
- Analytical and precise
- Evidence-based reasoning
- Critical perspective
- Scholarly references

### Hobby
- Enthusiastic and passionate
- Practical and hands-on
- Creative and exploratory
- Community-oriented

## Example Adaptations

**Same book (e.g., "Deep Work" by Cal Newport), different contexts:**

**Business context:** "Implement focused work blocks to increase team productivity by 30%"
**Personal context:** "Create a daily deep work ritual to reclaim your attention and find more meaning"
**Academic context:** "Apply Newport's concentration framework to research methodology"
**Hobby context:** "Use deep work principles to master your craft and create your best work"
